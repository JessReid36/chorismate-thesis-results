KEY FINDINGS:
 - The enzyme's STRONGEST electrostatic positions (Arg217, Arg134; largest |Dv|) AGREE with the
   barrier-lowering optimum: genuine transition-state-stabilising cations, which the design rediscovers
   (a designed +1 lands 2.06 A from Arg217).
 - Arg63 and Lys60 DISAGREE at moderate magnitude: the substrate's differential field wants ANIONS
   there, but the enzyme placed cations. These are exactly the positions where the net-free design
   places anions (6.4), and are the source of its barrier-lowering edge over net-neutral (Stage 4).
 - Interpretation: Arg63/Lys60 are plausibly present for substrate BINDING (chorismate is a -2 dianion;
   arginines anchor its carboxylates) rather than differential TS electrostatics - a residue serving a
   binding role at an electrostatic cost to the barrier. The method thus decomposes the active site
   into genuinely catalytic positions (rediscovered) and positions serving other roles (flagged by
   electrostatic disagreement).
