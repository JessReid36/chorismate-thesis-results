FINDINGS (real geometry, polarized):
 - Both designs beat the enzyme's ~9.1 kcal/mol even after polarization (net-neutral -12.2, net-free
   -14.0). Central claim (optimal static field matches/exceeds the enzyme) HOLDS on real geometry, polarized.
 - Net-neutral frozen-density holds cleanly (12%): the certified linear optimum is quantitatively
   meaningful for the dipolar design.
 - Net-free gives a larger raw effect (-14.0) but with a 23% correction: the strong net +2 field
   polarizes the substrate more, so the linear ddE=sum(q.Dv) model is rougher there.
 - METHODOLOGICAL CONCLUSION: net-neutral is the more DEFENSIBLE primary design (assumptions hold,
   12% vs 23%). Net-free reportable as "larger effect achievable with a net-charged field, at the cost
   of a larger polarization correction." Ties to novelty scope: certified linear optimization is exact
   in the frozen-density limit, which holds well for net-neutral, less well for the strong net-free field.
